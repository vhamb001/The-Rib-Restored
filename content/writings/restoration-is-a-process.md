We often pray for instant healing and immediate restoration. We want the shattered pieces to be whole again by morning. But true restoration—the kind that heals deep within and builds a foundation that cannot be shaken—is a process.

Think of a rib being restored. It requires time, precision, and the breath of life. It is not a quick fix; it is a profound remaking. Restoration means looking at the brokenness, acknowledging the pain, and allowing divine hands to meticulously reshape what was lost.

Do not rush your healing. Do not bypass the quiet seasons where the real work is done. You are being restored to purpose, and that purpose is far too important to be rushed. You are becoming exactly who you were always meant to be.
