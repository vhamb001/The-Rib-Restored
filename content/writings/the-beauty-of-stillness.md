In a world that constantly demands our attention and movement, stillness can feel like a contradiction. We are often told that to succeed, to build, and to impact, we must be in constant motion. But the truth of the kingdom is that true power is cultivated in the quiet.

Stillness is not an absence of action; it is an alignment of the heart. It is the posture of a watchwoman waiting on the wall, the silence of a dreamer listening for the download. When we quiet our minds and still our hands, we make room for the divine voice to speak.

In these quiet moments, the noise fades, and clarity emerges. Restoration begins not when we are striving, but when we are resting in the presence of our Creator. Remember, even the strongest rivers originate from calm, hidden springs. Embrace the beauty of stillness.
